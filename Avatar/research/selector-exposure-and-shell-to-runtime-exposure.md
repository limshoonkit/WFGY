# 🧪 Selector Exposure and Shell-to-Runtime Exposure

> Seeing more is not the same as ruling more.  
> In WFGY 5.0 Avatar, selector exposure and shell-to-runtime exposure exist so bounded observability, replay contrast, and constrained-lab inspection can happen without laundering exposure into routing sovereignty, runtime authority, or fake maturity.

**Quick links:** [Research Hub](./README.md) · [Master Toggle Map and Central TXT Toggle Block](./master-toggle-map-and-central-txt-toggle-block.md) · [Protocol Layer and Control Precedence](./protocol-layer-and-control-precedence.md) · [Return-Path Rebind Controls](./return-path-rebind-controls.md) · [Launchpad Front Door and Command Grammar](./launchpad-front-door-and-command-grammar.md) · [Architecture Overview](./architecture-overview.md) · [Blackfan Testing](../eval/blackfan-testing.md)

## 🧭 Why this page exists

Exposure is one of the easiest places to fake maturity.

A surface becomes visible.
A lab-facing field gets a name.
A replay can now see more structure.
A shell-facing note can now describe more of the runtime corridor.

And then a weak reader starts telling a dangerous story:

1. if the selector is exposable, it must be stable enough to trust directly
2. if shell-to-runtime exposure exists, the runtime is probably safe to flatten into a readable surface
3. if a family is visible, it must already be fit for public-facing simplification
4. if replay can inspect it, the underlying thing must already be mature enough to govern
5. if exposure exists, deeper routing or runtime law probably matters less

The packed master explicitly rejects that story.

That is why both `selector_exposure` and `shell_to_runtime_exposure` are preserved as **constrained-lab** families rather than promoted into universally stable public-bounded control families.

This page exists to stop exposure from turning into fake sovereignty.

## 🎯 Core claim

The core claim is simple.

Selector exposure and shell-to-runtime exposure are lawful visibility surfaces for bounded replay, comparison, and lab inspection.

They are not routing sovereignty, not runtime replacement, and not public-bounded maturity claims.

This means several things at once.

First, exposure is real.

Second, exposure is useful.

Third, exposure is bounded.

Fourth, exposure is non-sovereign.

Fifth, exposure may reveal more without authorizing more.

That is the center of this page.

## 🧩 What selector exposure actually is

Selector exposure is the bounded visibility surface through which selector behavior may be observed, contrasted, and inspected under constrained-lab conditions.

Its lawful purposes include:

1. replay comparison
2. lab contrast
3. bounded observability
4. selector-behavior inspection
5. regression visibility
6. exposure of selector-side experimental posture where lawfully permitted

This matters because selector-related behavior is especially easy to mythologize.

A system can expose selector behavior and then people start imagining:

1. the selector is now directly user-governable
2. the selector is now fully stable as a public routing organ
3. the selector may now self-advertise as a final router

The packed master explicitly blocks those inferences.

---

## 🚫 Selector exposure is not selector sovereignty

This boundary has to be stated directly.

Selector exposure does **not** mean:

1. selector now outranks the runtime body
2. selector now outranks constitutional law
3. selector now outranks hard control
4. selector may silently become a final router
5. selector experimentation may market itself as stable final authority

This matters because selector exposure is especially vulnerable to fake maturity.

Once a selector-like surface becomes visible, people start trusting it as if visibility itself were proof of readiness.

The packed master explicitly refuses that shortcut.

---

## 🔬 Why selector exposure remains constrained_lab

The packed master keeps `selector_exposure` in `constrained_lab` for a reason.

That reason is not weakness theater.
It is honesty.

Selector exposure remains constrained-lab because it may lawfully support:

1. replay inspection
2. contrast testing
3. bounded lab observation
4. selector-side experimental visibility

But it may not yet lawfully claim:

1. universal stable public routing maturity
2. sovereign authority over the runtime corridor
3. release-ready final router status
4. unrestricted public simplification

This is one of the most important anti-inflation moves in the whole control plane.

## 🧠 What shell-to-runtime exposure actually is

Shell-to-runtime exposure is the bounded visibility surface through which the relation between shell-facing entry behavior and runtime-side posture may be inspected without pretending that the runtime has been flattened into a readable shell summary.

Its lawful purposes include:

1. observing shell-entry to runtime transition behavior
2. bounded replay inspection of shell-to-runtime linkage
3. lab-facing comparison of shell-side phrasing and deeper runtime posture
4. limited observability into how shell-facing entry relates to runtime layers
5. debugging and regression inspection of transition mismatch

This matters because shell-facing language is one of the strongest counterfeiters of deeper structure.

A clean shell can make readers believe the runtime is now basically understood.
The packed master blocks that false comfort.

---

## 🚫 Shell-to-runtime exposure is not runtime transparency

This is the page’s second hard boundary.

Shell-to-runtime exposure does **not** mean:

1. full runtime transparency
2. runtime law has now been flattened into surface readability
3. shell-facing explanation now equals runtime truth
4. the shell may claim sovereignty over what the runtime is
5. public-facing simplified description may replace deeper runtime posture

That is exactly why the family stays constrained-lab.

The packed master allows bounded exposure.
It does not allow transparency theater.

## 🔽 Why shell-to-runtime exposure also remains constrained_lab

The master keeps `shell_to_runtime_exposure` in `constrained_lab` because shell-runtime linkage is especially vulnerable to overinterpretation.

A visible shell-to-runtime story can tempt readers to believe:

1. runtime posture is now safely explainable in a simplified public form
2. bounded shell description can now stand in for deeper routing and posture truth
3. runtime seams are now small enough to ignore
4. surface explanation equals structural adequacy

The packed master explicitly rejects those inferences.

So this family remains constrained-lab because it is lawful for:

1. lab observation
2. replay contrast
3. regression visibility
4. bounded debugging

but not for fake runtime simplification.

## 🧷 Selector exposure and shell-to-runtime exposure are not interchangeable

This page also has to keep these two families separate.

Selector exposure asks:
what bounded visibility into selector-side behavior is lawful?

Shell-to-runtime exposure asks:
what bounded visibility into shell-entry to runtime linkage is lawful?

These are related, but not the same.

A weak system can blur them and start talking vaguely about “deeper internal visibility.”
The packed master refuses that blur.

One concerns selector-side bounded lab observability.
The other concerns shell-to-runtime linkage observability.
They must remain distinct.

## 🚫 Exposure does not rewrite routing authority

Another hard law:

neither exposure family may rewrite routing authority.

That means:

1. selector exposure may not promote the selector into final router sovereignty
2. shell-to-runtime exposure may not let shell narratives dictate runtime truth
3. observability may not become routing authority
4. replay visibility may not become decision permission
5. lab exposure may not silently override routing law

This matters because routing authority is easy to counterfeit with a strong-looking observability surface.
The packed master explicitly blocks that route.

## 🚫 Exposure does not rewrite runtime truth

The same law holds on the runtime side.

Shell-to-runtime exposure may reveal useful bounded information about shell/runtime relation.

1. compress runtime truth into a shell summary
2. replace deeper runtime posture
3. erase runtime-state complexity by interface readability
4. convert public explanation into law-bearing equivalence

This is one of the most important anti-simplification rules in the whole page.

The system is allowed to describe linkage.
It is not allowed to collapse the runtime into the description.

This matters because advanced exposure often feels like deeper truth.

The packed master keeps that order explicit.
