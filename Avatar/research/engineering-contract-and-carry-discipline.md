## 🎯 Core claim

The core claim is simple.

Engineering contract is the law that governs how parent-grade body may enter carried operational forms without lying about preservation.

Carry discipline is the law that governs how that carried form remains usable without claiming substitution, equivalence, or erased asymmetry.

This means several things at once.

First, engineering handling is real.

Second, engineering handling is not sovereign.

Third, carry is lawful.

Fourth, carry is not the same thing as replacing the body.

Fifth, operational success never cancels theorem-facing restraint, validation hardening, or later accountability.

That is why Part 9 exists.

Without it, engineering success would be free to counterfeit structural truth.

The packed master is explicit here.

Engineering law may not remain only implied.

If engineering contract were left at the level of convenience prose, several illegal compressions would become easy:

1. child artifacts could quietly impersonate parent-grade identity

Without it, later implementation would look practical but remain lawlessly vague.

## 🧾 Engineering contract identity

The engineering contract identifies the lawful terms of transfer from parent-grade body into carried operational forms.

Its role is to preserve, at minimum:

1. parent-grade body remains prior
2. bounded carried forms may still be useful
3. carried forms may expose utility without pretending total equivalence
4. engineering smoothness may not erase structural truth
5. the user-facing usefulness of a carried form remains answerable to body-preservation honesty

This matters because engineering contract is not merely a “format agreement.”
It is a truth-preservation agreement under bounded usability.

That is why the word contract matters here.

It binds what may be claimed about the carried form.

## 🎒 Carry discipline identity

Carry discipline is the part of Part 9 that governs what it means to lawfully move body into usable downstream form.

Carry discipline exists because without it, carrying is easy to overclaim.

A system can say:

1. this artifact is based on the parent
2. this export supports the parent
3. this child build reflects the parent

And all of those phrases can hide very different realities.

Carry discipline forces a stronger distinction between:

1. what is still present
2. what is reduced
3. what is only boundedly represented
4. what remains unavailable in the carried form
5. what may not be claimed despite operational usefulness

This is one of the strongest honesty structures in the later body.

---

## 🚫 Carry is not full-body substitution

This is one of the sharpest laws in all of Part 9.

Carry is not full-body substitution.

That means:

1. a carried artifact may remain useful
2. a carried artifact may remain faithful in bounded ways
3. a carried artifact may still be non-equivalent to the parent
4. a carried artifact may not erase what was lost
5. a carried artifact may not overwrite parent-grade priority

This matters because substitution is one of the easiest lies for engineering success to tell.

Something works.
Something exports.
Something connects.
Something is readable.
Something is usable.

And a weak system starts acting as if parent and child are now effectively the same.

Part 9 explicitly rejects that move.

---

## 🔽 Parent-child asymmetry remains binding

The packed master is equally clear that parent-child asymmetry remains explicit.

That means:

1. the parent remains prior
2. the child remains derived
3. the child may be lawful without being equivalent
4. the child may inherit in bounded ways without inheriting everything
5. the child may remain useful without cancelling asymmetry

This matters because one of the strongest engineering illusions is symmetry theater.

A child artifact that behaves well enough can begin to look like an equal body.
Part 9 rejects that illusion.

Usability is not identity.
Inheritance is not symmetry.
Derived usefulness is not parent-grade status.

Carry discipline is also explicitly downstream of theorem-facing restraint.

This matters because engineering success can look so persuasive that readers begin to think proof burden has shrunk.
