# 🧷 Parent-Child Asymmetry and Bounded Export

> A useful child object is not the parent body in smaller clothes.  
> In WFGY 5.0 Avatar, parent-child asymmetry and bounded export exist so carried, exported, summarized, shell-facing, and machine-readable forms may remain useful without counterfeit equivalence, counterfeit authority, or counterfeit completion.

**Quick links:** [Research Hub](./README.md) · [Architecture Overview](./architecture-overview.md) · [Packed Master Structure Map](./packed-master-structure-map.md) · [Engineering Contract and Carry Discipline](./engineering-contract-and-carry-discipline.md) · [Transport Discipline and Compatibility Law](./transport-discipline-and-compatibility-law.md) · [Matrix Accountability and Numeric Binding](./matrix-accountability-and-numeric-binding.md) · [Readiness Constitution and Acceptance Boundary](./readiness-constitution-and-acceptance-boundary.md) · [Blackfan Testing](../eval/blackfan-testing.md)

## 🧭 Why this page exists

One of the easiest technical lies is not loud at all.

Something exports cleanly.
Something loads into a tool.
Something becomes readable for a shell surface.
Something becomes compact enough for a child artifact.
Something becomes machine-readable enough for downstream use.

And then a weak system starts telling itself a very dangerous story:

1. this is close enough to the parent
2. this probably preserves the important parts anyway
3. this is what people will actually use, so it is effectively the real thing
4. the asymmetry probably no longer matters
5. the export seems stable, so deeper loss probably is not important

The packed master explicitly rejects that story.

That is why parent-child asymmetry and bounded export must remain explicit.
Without this page, a lot of later docs would slowly drift into one silent fraud:

**useful enough becomes equal enough.**

That move is unlawful here.

## 🎯 Core claim

The core claim is simple.

A child artifact may be lawful, useful, readable, exportable, machine-readable, and even highly practical, while still remaining non-equivalent to the parent body.

That sentence does a lot of work.

It means:

1. usefulness is allowed
2. bounded export is allowed
3. compact forms are allowed
4. derived surfaces are allowed

But it also means:

1. usefulness does not erase asymmetry
2. portability does not erase reduction
3. compatibility does not erase non-equivalence
4. readability does not erase boundedness
5. export-readiness does not erase acceptance boundary

That is the heart of this page.

## 🧱 Why parent-child asymmetry must stay explicit

Parent-child asymmetry is not a nice theoretical reminder.
It is one of the main anti-fraud laws in the later body.

If asymmetry becomes merely implied, then all of the following slides become easier:

1. child convenience starts impersonating parent completeness
2. summary posture starts impersonating whole-body preservation
3. shell-readable surfaces start impersonating legal authority
4. matrix-readable surfaces start impersonating the body they summarize
5. bounded exports start impersonating proof of total continuity

That is exactly why the packed master keeps asymmetry explicit.

Asymmetry says:

1. the parent remains prior
2. the child remains derived
3. the child may inherit boundedly
4. the child may remain useful
5. the child may not erase the parent’s precedence

That is not mood.
That is law.

## 🏛️ Parent body remains source-of-truth prior

The parent body remains the prior legal object.

This means:

1. parent-grade law remains upstream
2. parent-grade protected organs remain upstream
3. parent-grade closure posture remains upstream
4. child surfaces may expose bounded readability
5. child surfaces may not redefine the source-of-truth relation

This is one of the most important distinctions in the whole engineering and export region.

If the source-of-truth relation disappears, bounded export becomes almost impossible to keep honest.

Once that happens, every smooth child form begins to look like a replacement.

The packed master explicitly blocks that route.

## 👶 Child usefulness is lawful, but bounded

A child object is not illegal just because it is smaller.

A child object may lawfully be:

1. compact
2. shell-readable
3. export-safe
4. machine-readable
5. tool-usable
6. summary-bearing

That permissiveness matters.
The system is not anti-usability.

But the child remains bounded in at least four ways:

1. bounded by carried scope
2. bounded by source-of-truth asymmetry
3. bounded by theorem-facing restraint
4. bounded by validation and support honesty

So the child may be practical.
It may not become sovereign.

## 📦 Bounded export identity

Bounded export is the lawful movement of reduced structure into a usable outward form under explicit preservation of boundedness.

That sentence matters because bounded export is not just “export.”

It is export under law.

Its lawful function is to allow:

1. readable external surfaces
2. machine-readable continuation surfaces
3. audit-facing bounded surfaces
4. child-facing bounded artifacts
5. shell-facing bounded guidance surfaces

But bounded export also preserves the fact that:

1. not everything travels
2. not everything remains equally exposed
3. not everything remains equally authoritative
4. reduction remains real
5. parent priority remains intact

That is why the word bounded cannot be removed without losing the law itself.

## ✅ What bounded export may lawfully carry

A bounded export may carry forms such as:

1. bounded runtime posture
2. bounded route posture
3. bounded support posture
4. bounded claim posture
5. bounded compatibility posture
6. bounded audit readability
7. bounded continuation hooks
8. bounded summary-layer identity where lawful

This matters because the system is not saying exports should be empty.
They may carry real value.

But that value must remain lawful value.
It may not silently claim to carry the whole body in full.

---

## 🚫 What bounded export may not claim

A bounded export may **not** lawfully claim:

1. full constitutional body
2. full formal spine
3. full admissibility burden
4. full controller history
5. full SRD family / unit / diagnostics reality
6. full engineering contract body
7. full matrix-bearing accountability body
8. final theorem-facing adequacy

This is one of the strongest anti-hallucination rules in the later body.

The export may be useful.
It may be clear.
It may be stable.
It may be highly valuable.

It still may not lie about what it is not.

## 🔗 Compatibility without equivalence

The packed master is very direct here.

Compatibility is real.
Equivalence is not implied.

That means:

1. a child artifact may lawfully interoperate
2. an export may lawfully support downstream tools
3. a shell-facing view may lawfully support weak-reader orientation
4. a matrix-facing surface may lawfully support accountability readability

None of those imply:

1. no loss occurred
2. no asymmetry remains
3. no boundedness remains
4. no parent priority remains
5. no further law is needed upstream

This is one of the most important distinctions in the whole page.

Without it, “works well enough” immediately becomes “is the same thing.”

The packed master rejects that shortcut.

## 🪟 Bounded export readability is not body identity

Part 9A explicitly preserves bounded export readability.

That means later accountability surfaces may expose readable, bounded forms of:

1. validation posture
2. claim-boundary posture
3. authority-formalization posture
4. reduction posture
5. reconciliation posture

That is lawful.

But the same law also says these bounded readable forms are not themselves the bodies they expose.

This matters because matrix readability is persuasive.
Once it looks clean enough, readers start forgetting the difference between:

1. readable exposure
2. legal organ
3. full parent body

This page exists to keep those three apart.

## 🤖 Machine-readable continuation is still bounded

Machine-readable forms are especially easy to over-trust.

Something loads cleanly.
Something parses.
Something survives tooling.
Something becomes reusable across workflows.

And a weak system starts imagining that machine-readability equals portable completeness.

The packed master explicitly rejects that.

Machine-readable continuation may lawfully support:

1. bounded continuation
2. bounded hooks
3. bounded downstream interoperability
4. bounded audit readability

But it may not lawfully imply:

1. whole-body portability
2. whole-body equivalence
3. whole-body authority inheritance
4. final closure

Machine-readability is useful.
It is not sovereign.

---

## 📐 Bounded export remains downstream of theorem-facing honesty

This is one of the most important downstream checks in the whole page.

Bounded export remains answerable to theorem-facing honesty.

That means:

1. export stability may not imply proof completion
2. export readability may not imply closure
3. compact law-bearing surfaces may not claim more closure than the parent has lawfully earned
4. child neatness may not elevate theorem-facing maturity

This matters because export polish is one of the strongest counterfeiters of finality.

A good export can make a system look more finished than it actually is.
The packed master blocks that inflation explicitly.

---

## ✅ Bounded export remains downstream of validation hardening

Bounded export also remains downstream of validation hardening.

That means:

1. support class may not be upgraded through export polish
2. partial support may not be disguised as mature support
3. downgrade-sensitive posture may not be smoothed away
4. redirect-sensitive posture may not be hidden by interface convenience
5. unsupported structure may not become supported merely because it exported well

This matters because people trust clean interfaces too much.

A neat export often receives more trust than the parent body actually earned.
The packed master forces that trust to stay bounded.

## 🚩 Export-readiness is not acceptance

The readiness constitution already says export-readiness is a real readiness class.

That matters.
This page keeps it honest.

Export-readiness means:

1. bounded outward forms may now be lawfully produced or supported
2. those forms may be usable
3. those forms may be practical
4. those forms may be stable enough for bounded downstream use

Export-readiness does **not** mean:

1. final acceptance
2. parent-grade equivalence
3. final audit completion
4. theorem-grade closure
5. erased open items

This is exactly why the readiness page and this page need to sit beside one another.

4. a machine-readable form does not prove whole-body portability
5. a child package that “works” does not prove acceptance

This matters because export success is one of the strongest counterfeiters of done.

The packed master explicitly rejects that false signal.

That means:

## 🌱 Anti-deadness discipline

The packed master also refuses dead export formalism.

That means:

1. bounded export may remain useful and alive

4. living usability and strict boundedness may coexist

This matters because some systems become dishonest through softness.

This page refuses both.
