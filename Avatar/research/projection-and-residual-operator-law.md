> Projection is not a metaphor for attention, and projected residual is not poetic residue.

## 🧭 Why this page exists

Projection is one of the easiest formal words to fake.

At a weak level, people hear “projection” and think it means:

1. focus on what matters
2. summarize the relevant parts
3. simplify the state
4. ignore the rest for now

That reading is too weak.

In the packed master, projection is not a motivational idea about relevance.
It is a formal operator body.
And projected residual is not “what remains unsaid.”
It is a lawful remainder-bearing structure.

Without this page, readers can easily collapse Part 5C into one of the following weaker readings:

1. a metaphor for selective attention
2. a prose explanation for reduction
3. a style note about concise thinking
4. a compression convenience note
5. a way to make later controller actions look cleaner than their inputs really were

This page exists to stop that collapse.

## 🎯 Core claim

The core claim is simple.

Projection is the lawful reduction operator that maps broader available structure into current admissible relevance under the active admissibility frame.

Projected residual is the lawful remainder-bearing structure that records what was not admitted into current immediate use but also was not lawfully erased.

This means several things at once.

First, projection is not deletion.

Second, reduction is not exhaustion.

Third, remainder is not stylistic noise.

Fourth, later controller, realization, and theorem-facing claims may not pretend that a clean projected state means the deeper structure is gone.

That is why projection and projected residual belong to body law rather than to compression rhetoric.

## 🧮 The canonical projection operator

The packed master preserves the canonical phase-aware projection operator:

`Pi_H_p(z_t)`

where:

1. `z_t` denotes broader observed or available engine state at the relevant active point
2. `H_p` denotes the active phase-conditioned admissibility frame
3. `Pi_H_p(z_t)` denotes lawful reduction of broader available structure into current admissible relevance

This notation matters because it stops projection from dissolving into loose prose.

The operator says something precise:

1. broader state still exists
2. admissibility is active
3. reduction is conditioned by that admissibility frame
4. what becomes current use is not arbitrary
5. lawful reduction still owes honesty to what did not become current use

So the operator is not decorative math.
It is a legality-bearing way to stop “current focus” from turning into silent deletion.
