## 🧭 Why this page exists

A protocol surface is one of the easiest places to counterfeit power.

The controls look more technical.
The fields look more granular.
The sequencing looks more advanced.
The operator feels like they are now “closer to the real system.”

And then a weak reader starts telling a dangerous story:

1. advanced controls must be closer to sovereignty
2. protocol fields probably outrank the launchpad
3. replay visibility probably implies permission
4. debug visibility probably implies authority
5. if precedence is explicit, legality must already be settled

The packed master explicitly rejects that story.

That is why the protocol layer is preserved as a bounded advanced control surface with explicit precedence and explicit seals. It exists to gather already-answerable control handles into one advanced surface, not to invent a new authority layer and not to turn observability into permission.
